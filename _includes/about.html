About Me

Portrait @ Nikolaus Brade

I am a doctoral candidate in developmental neuroscience and psychiatry and have a background in medicine. In my work, I focus on atypical neurobiological processes involved in developmental aspects of psychiatric disorders. To study these, I use integrative data analysis methods to map psychiatric traits and disorders at multiple biological levels, from behavioral to brain regional to molecular and genetic processes.

I am currently participating in a four-year interdisciplinary Clinician Scientist PhD-program at the Max Planck School of Cognition and am affiliated with Jürgen Dukart's Biomarker Development Group at Jülich Research Centre.
