About Me

I am a junior researcher in developmental neuroscience and psychiatry with a background in medicine. In my work, I focus on atypical neurobiological processes involved in developmental aspects of psychiatric disorders. To study these, I use integrative data analysis methods to map psychiatric traits and disorders at multiple biological levels, from behavioral to brain regional to molecular and genetic processes.

I take part in a 4-year interdisciplinary Clinician Scientist Program of the Max Planck School of Cognition, Germany, and am affiliated with Jürgen Dukart's Biomarker Development Group at the Jülich Research Centre.
